Imposter – Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Imposter ("the App", "we", "us") handles your information. By using the App, you agree to this policy.

1. Information We Collect

  • Device Information: Device model, OS version, and unique device identifiers, used for analytics and bug fixes.
  • Gameplay Data: In-game actions, scores, and session statistics to improve gameplay experience.
  • Crash Reports: Diagnostic data sent automatically when the app crashes, to help us fix issues.
  • Usage Analytics: Anonymous data about how features are used (e.g., screens visited, buttons tapped).

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information unless you voluntarily contact us.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To operate and improve the App
  • To diagnose crashes and fix bugs
  • To analyze usage patterns and enhance features
  • To respond to support requests you send us

3. Third-Party Services

The App may use third-party services including:

  • Apple Game Center – for leaderboards and multiplayer features (subject to Apple's Privacy Policy)
  • Apple App Store – for distribution and in-app purchases, if applicable
  • Analytics providers – anonymous usage data only, no personally identifiable information shared

We do not sell your data to third parties.

4. Data Storage and Security

Any data collected is stored securely. Gameplay and analytics data is retained only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described above. We use industry-standard practices to protect your data, but no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

5. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

6. Your Rights

You may request deletion of any personal data associated with you by contacting us at the email below. Because we collect minimal identifying data, most usage data is anonymous and cannot be linked back to an individual.
